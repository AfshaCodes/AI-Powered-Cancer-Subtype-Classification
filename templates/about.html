üéØ Overview

This application uses advanced deep learning to classify breast cancer into distinct molecular subtypes based on gene expression data. By analyzing patterns across 5,000 genes, our Variational Autoencoder (VAE) model can identify cancer subtypes that respond differently to treatment.

üî¨ The Science

Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) are a type of neural network that learns to compress high-dimensional data into a compact representation. In our case, we compress 5,000 gene expression values into a 50-dimensional "latent space" that captures the essential biological patterns.

Clustering in this latent space using K-Means reveals groups of patients with similar molecular profiles. These clusters correspond to different cancer subtypes with distinct clinical outcomes.
